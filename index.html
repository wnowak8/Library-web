Our history

The Amelia Gayle Gorgas Library, designed by Birmingham architect Hugh Martin in 1939, is located in the center of the north side of the Main Quadrangle, facing the President’s Mansion. Thoroughly trained in the Beaux-Arts tradition, Martin created a finely detailed structure that related both in style (Classical Revival) and scale to the other buildings surrounding the quadrangle. At the same time, the library building literally turns it back to the Victorian structures in Woods Quadrangle. Martin sited the building so that the massive entrance stairs rest precisely on the northern half of the stone foundations of the antebellum Rotunda that served as the university’s first library.
